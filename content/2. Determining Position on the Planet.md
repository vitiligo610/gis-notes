### Latitude
The **Equator** is perpendicular to the axis of the Earth and splits the planet into the North and South hemispheres. Latitude helps determine how far north or south from the equator you are.

*   Latitude is an **angular measurement** from the equator to the North or South pole.
*   Latitude lines (parallels) are **equidistant** and parallel to each other.
*   $1^\circ$ of latitude $\approx 69 \text{ miles}$.

**Key Latitudes:**
*   $0^\circ \rightarrow$ Equator
*   $23.5^\circ \text{ N} \rightarrow$ Tropic of Cancer
*   $23.5^\circ \text{ S} \rightarrow$ Tropic of Capricorn
*   $66.5^\circ \text{ N} \rightarrow$ Arctic Circle
*   $66.5^\circ \text{ S} \rightarrow$ Antarctic Circle

**Calculation of Latitude:**
$\theta = 90^\circ - \text{Sun Angle} + \text{Sun's Declination}$

### Longitude
Longitude measures how far East or West you are from an arbitrary starting point.

*   Today, the arbitrary $0^\circ$ point is the **Prime Meridian**.
*   Longitude goes East up to $180^\circ \text{ E}$ and West up to $180^\circ \text{ W}$.
    *   $0^\circ \rightarrow$ Prime Meridian
    *   $180^\circ \rightarrow$ International Date Line
*   Unlike latitude, longitude lines are **not parallel**. They are furthest apart at the equator and converge at the poles.
*   At the equator, $1^\circ \text{ of longitude} \approx 69 \text{ miles}$.
*   Lines of longitude are called **Meridians**.
*   **Chronometer:** A sea clock historically used to determine longitude.
*   **Relationship between Longitude & Time:** 
    $\frac{360^\circ}{24 \text{ hrs}} = 15^\circ \text{ of longitude per hour}$

> **Note:** A **Graticule** is the grid network formed by the combination of parallels (latitude) and meridians (longitude).

### Coordinate Notation
*   **DMS Notation:** Degrees, Minutes, Seconds ($1^\circ = 60'$ and $1' = 60''$)
*   **DD Notation:** Decimal Degrees
*   **Programming / Library Standards:** 
    *   Positive (+): North, East
    *   Negative (-): South, West
